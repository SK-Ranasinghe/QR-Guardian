# QR Guardian User Manual

## 1. Introduction

QR Guardian is a mobile security app designed to help you scan QR codes safely before opening their content. It checks links and QR payloads for suspicious behaviour, warns you about risky actions, and gives you extra security insights when needed.

## 2. Main Features

- Secure QR code scanning
- Safety rating for scanned content
- AI-based security analysis
- VirusTotal deep scan
- Domain intelligence lookup
- Scan history tracking
- Trusted sites management
- Security dashboard
- Notification alerts
- Biometric app protection

## 3. Getting Started

### 3.1 Open the app

When you launch QR Guardian:

- you may be asked to unlock the app using biometrics if enabled on your device
- you may be asked to allow camera access so the app can scan QR codes
- you may also enable notifications for alerts and weekly updates

### 3.2 Permissions used by the app

QR Guardian may request access to:

- **Camera**: to scan QR codes
- **Notifications**: to send threat alerts, weekly reports, and security tips
- **Biometric authentication**: to protect app access on supported devices

## 4. How to Scan a QR Code

1. Open the **Scanner** tab.
2. Point your camera at the QR code.
3. Hold the device steady until the code is detected.
4. Wait briefly while the app analyzes the QR content.
5. Review the result screen before taking any action.

## 5. Understanding the Result Screen

After each scan, QR Guardian shows a result screen with a safety assessment.

### 5.1 Safety ratings

- **SAFE**: the scanned content appears low risk
- **CAUTION**: the content may contain suspicious signs and should be reviewed carefully
- **DANGEROUS**: the content shows clear warning signs and should not be trusted

### 5.2 What you will see

The result screen may show:

- the scanned URL or payload
- the overall safety rating
- the score assigned by the app
- warning reasons or detected issues
- options for deeper analysis

### 5.3 Opening scanned content

QR Guardian does not blindly open risky content.

- safer results can be opened more directly
- lower-confidence or risky results may show a warning confirmation first
- phone or SMS actions are also treated carefully and are not opened automatically without user awareness

## 6. Advanced Analysis Options

The result screen can provide additional security checks.

### 6.1 AI Analysis

Use **AI Analysis** when you want a plain-language security interpretation of the scanned content.

This can help explain:

- whether the content looks suspicious
- what kind of threat it may represent
- why it may be risky

### 6.2 VirusTotal Scan

Use **VirusTotal Scan** for a deeper external reputation check.

This helps you see:

- whether security engines detect the link as malicious or suspicious
- how many engines flagged it
- whether the link appears widely trusted or risky

### 6.3 Domain Intelligence

Use **Domain Intelligence** to inspect background information about a website.

This may include:

- domain age
- registrar information
- country information
- whether the domain appears very new

Newly registered domains can sometimes indicate increased risk.

## 7. Trusted Sites

The **Trusted Sites** section lets you manage websites you personally trust.

You can:

- add trusted domains from the result screen
- view saved trusted sites
- remove sites you no longer want to trust
- clear the full trusted list if needed

Use this feature carefully. Only trust domains you are fully confident about.

## 8. Scan History

The **History** section stores your recent scans locally on the device.

You can:

- review previous scan results
- see the rating and issues for each scan
- delete individual entries
- clear the full history

This is useful for tracking suspicious links you scanned earlier.

## 9. Dashboard

The **Dashboard** gives a summary of your scanning activity.

It can help you view:

- total scans over time
- safe versus risky scan patterns
- activity trends
- overall security behaviour

This is useful for understanding your QR scanning habits.

## 10. Notifications

The **Notifications** section lets you control alert settings.

Types of notifications may include:

- **Threat alerts**: when previously scanned content becomes more risky
- **Weekly reports**: summary of your scanning activity
- **Security tips**: short reminders to improve safe behaviour

You can turn notifications on or off from the app settings screen.

## 11. Biometric Protection

On supported devices, QR Guardian can use biometric authentication to protect access to the app.

This helps ensure that:

- only the device owner can access scan records and security tools
- sensitive scan history is protected from casual access

If biometric support is unavailable on the device, the app can still open normally.

## 12. Safe Usage Tips

To use QR Guardian effectively:

- always review the result before opening any link
- be extra careful with QR codes found in public places
- avoid scanning stickers placed over original QR codes
- be cautious with codes that trigger calls, messages, or Wi-Fi setup
- treat newly registered or strange-looking domains with suspicion
- do not rely only on appearance; always check the rating and warnings

## 13. Troubleshooting

### Camera is not working

- make sure camera permission is allowed on your device
- restart the app and try again
- check whether another app is using the camera

### Notifications are not arriving

- confirm notifications are enabled in the app
- confirm notification permission is allowed in device settings
- make sure battery-saving settings are not blocking alerts

### AI or external scans are unavailable

- check your internet connection
- some advanced checks depend on external services and may take longer or be temporarily unavailable

## 14. Conclusion

QR Guardian is designed to reduce the risk of blindly trusting QR codes. By combining QR scanning, security analysis, threat intelligence, and user warnings, it helps users make safer decisions before opening suspicious content.
